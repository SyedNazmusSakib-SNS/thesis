Lack of Structured Representation Learning

Literature Review | Slide 10

A Unstructured RRG Models

R2Gen (Chen et al.)

Memory-driven Transformer for report generation; produces coherent reports but relies on unstructured visual embeddings and token-level decoding.

Limitation: No explicit entity grounding or claim verification; prone to fluent but unsupported statements.

R2GenGPT / Visual–LLM Alignment (Wang et al.)

Aligns visual features with frozen LLMs to boost language fluency.

Limitation: Better fluency and alignment but still lacks systematic verification and temporal/longitudinal grounding.

B Structured or Graph-Based Efforts (but Static)

RadGraph (Jain et al.)

Expert-annotated entity–relation graphs from radiology reports enabling structured supervision.

Limitation: Graphs are text-derived and largely static; do not model entity evolution across exams nor ensure image-grounded claims.

Chest ImaGenome (Wu et al.)

Large scene-graph dataset linking image regions to structured tags and some comparison relations.

Limitation: Provides scene graphs but limited longitudinal alignment and limited end-to-end image-to-graph temporal reasoning in current models.
