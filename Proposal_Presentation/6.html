Problem Statement

Introduction | Slide 6

Primary Issue: Lack of Temporal Dynamics

Existing RRG systems lack explicit mechanisms to model inter-study differences. They treat longitudinal exams as independent "snapshots," ignoring the temporal context vital for diagnosis.

Consequence: Contradictory Reporting

Models produce contradictory progression statements (e.g., claiming "worsening" and "stable" simultaneously) and inaccurate stability claims, severely limiting clinical utility.

Technical Deficit

Current architectures rely on generic image embeddings without structured clinical reasoning or iterative verification steps to ensure factual consistency.
