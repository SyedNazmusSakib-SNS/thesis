Structured Graph Integration

Methodology | Slide 20

Building the Graph

Nodes = Anatomy & Findings

Representing distinct medical entities (e.g., "Left Lower Lobe", "Nodule").

Edges = Semantic Relations

Defining spatial and logical connections (e.g., located_in , adjacent_to ).

Temporal Alignment

We establish correspondence by matching nodes between the Prior Graph ( G p ) and the Current Graph ( G c ) to track specific anatomical regions over time.

Change Vectors

We compute a difference vector for specific nodes to quantify evolution (e.g., pleural effusion size):

Δg k = g k c − g k p
