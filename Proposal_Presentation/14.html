Verification Methods in Current RRG Models

Literature Review | Slide 12

What Current Models Do

  • Autoregressive Report Generation Models such as TieNet, R2Gen, CheXagent, and LLaVA-Rad generate reports token by token conditioned on visual embeddings.

  • Snapshot-Based Reasoning Most systems operate on a single study at a time, treating reports as image captions rather than clinical interpretations.

  • Implicit Visual Grounding Visual features are fused implicitly via attention, without explicit verification of individual clinical claims.

  • Text-Centric Optimization Training objectives primarily optimize language likelihood or text similarity metrics such as BLEU and ROUGE.

Key Limitations Identified in the Literature

  • No Fact Verification at Inference Time Generated statements are not checked against image evidence, allowing unsupported findings to pass unchallenged.

  • Hallucination-Prone Generation Prior work reports frequent false positives, incorrect device mentions, and unsupported severity claims.

  • Poor Temporal Reliability Longitudinal consistency is not enforced, leading to contradictory progression or stability statements.
