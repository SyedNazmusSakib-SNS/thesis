The Hallucination Problem in Depth

Literature Review | Slide 11

Medical VLMs are prone to generating unsupported findings. In radiology, these hallucinations are not just linguistic errors but represent significant clinical safety risks .

Hallucination Type Definition Clinical Risk Severity
False Positive
Reporting a finding (e.g., nodule) not present in the image. Unnecessary treatment, radiation exposure, anxiety. High
Incorrect Localization
Attributing finding to wrong region (e.g., Left vs Right). Procedural errors, incorrect surgical planning. High
Severity Distortion
Misrepresenting extent/size (e.g., "Mild" vs "Severe"). Misjudgment of urgency; delayed escalation. Medium
Device Hallucination
Fabricating medical devices (e.g., tubes) not present. Confusion about patient status (e.g., intubation). Medium
Temporal Hallucination
Fabricating change (e.g., "worsening") when stable. Critical longitudinal misinterpretation; wrong therapy. High
