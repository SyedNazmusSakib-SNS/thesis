Approaches to Longitudinal Reasoning

Literature Review | Slide 11

Current methods for analyzing temporal changes in medical imaging fall into two primary categories, both with significant limitations.

Feature-Level Strategies

Mechanism:

Simple concatenation of image features or computing pixel-level differences.

Limitation:

Lacks semantic interpretability; treats change as numerical noise.

Transformer-Based Models

Mechanism:

Utilizes self-attention to correlate features across different time steps.

Limitation:

Operates on unstructured embeddings, failing to align changes with anatomy.

The Research Gap

No system currently combines temporal attention with structured anatomical graphs to explicitly model disease progression.
