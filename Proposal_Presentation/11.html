Approaches to Longitudinal Reasoning

Literature Review | Slide 11

Current methods for analyzing temporal changes in medical imaging fall into two primary categories, both with significant limitations.

Feature-Level Strategies

Mechanism

Simple concatenation of image features or computing pixel-level differences between exams.

Limitation

Lacks semantic interpretability; treats change as numerical noise rather than clinical evolution.

Transformer-Based Models

Mechanism

Utilizes self-attention mechanisms to correlate features across different time steps.

Limitation

Operates on unstructured embeddings, failing to align changes with specific anatomical structures.

The Research Gap

No system currently combines temporal attention with structured anatomical graphs to explicitly model disease progression.
